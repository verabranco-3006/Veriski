Despite all this investment, 90% of transformative use cases are currently stuck in pilot mode (McKinsey) - why? 
Many believe that AI will deliver on its promises with the next generation model or even AGI (general artificial intelligence)
That assumption has put many companies into a state of “wait and see,” endless AI pilots and disconnected agentic experiments - they are trapped on the wrong side of the “enterprise gap”
But better AI is not actually going to help you bridge the gap to becoming a truly AI-powered, agentic enterprise 
Because AI is inherently a probabilistic technology that relies on statistical models to generate variable outputs (and can be incredibly prolific when it comes to generating content and code) - 
Real enterprise applications are deterministic - they follow predefined rules to produce predictable and reliable results you can trust for core processes and mission-critical operations 
So how do you get from AI experimentation to enterprise AI that is
Integrated with core systems?
Governed and compliant?
Reliable in production?
Predictable at scale?
The key to bridging the enterprise AI gap is to combine the probabilistic nature of AI to accelerate the build phase of development, with a deterministic framework to deliver trusted AI apps and solutions that behave according to your enterprise and industry requirements, every time.
