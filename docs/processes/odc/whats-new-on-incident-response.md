### ***Who leads incidents with system-wide impacts, and in which tool?***
The SRE Team will lead incidents **with system-wide impact** in Rootly, and teams will be added to Slack channels just like before. Nothing changes.

**Documentation - ** SCENARIO 2 - Incident with system-wide impact 

### ***Who leads incidents without system-wide impacts, and in which tool?***
The Development Teams will lead incidents without system-wide impact using Jira instead of Rootly.&nbsp;

**Documentation - **SCENARIO 1 - Incident without system-wide impact 

### ***What should Dev teams do if they detect a system-wide impact (late detection)?***
In case of **late detection of system-wide impact **incidents, the Development Teams must involve the SRE team using Jira. SRE then takes the lead.&nbsp;

**Documentation - **SCENARIO 3 - Incident with late detection of system-wide impact 

### ***If late detection system-wide incidents involve SRE via Jira, do they get moved to Rootly?***
All incidents will be instantiated in Jira and Rootly (regardless of the impact), and are kept synchronized at all times. As a Dev team member, moving from Jira to Rootly in a late detection scenario is something you don&rsquo;t need to worry about - just make sure to involve SRE to take the lead.

### ***What if an incident is happening and there is no RDINC generated automatically?***
You must generate it manually. Jira Incidents (RDINC) are the single source of records for Incident Response and if there is no automatic mechanism to create the incident (e.g. reported by customers, Grafana alerts, or SLOs), you must go to this form and create the incident manually. 

**Documentation - **Incident Response Process 

### ***What happens with retrospectives generated by Rootly?***
All **retrospectives** will be generated by Rootly (as it happens today), but they will now be linked to the corresponding Jira ticket for easier access by Development Teams. You can also generate a Retrospective, through Jira Incident Retrospective tab.

**Documentation - **Retrospective Process 

### ***Does anything else change regarding retrospectives?***
Yes! Starting December 13, all retrospectives** **will follow the same governance model as in Emergency Changes RCAs. This means that:

- 
Retrospectives will be produced in partnership between SRE and Dev teams;

- 
Retrospectives will be presented, peer-reviewed, and approved by the Value Stream Leaders involved;

- 
***New -*** The Process Engineering team will monitor all action items to ensure commitment and completeness.

**Documentation - **Retrospective Process 

### ***What happens if an Incident is moved to &ldquo;Resolved&rdquo; in Rootly, but not in Jira?***
It may occur that an Incident is marked as &ldquo;Resolved&rdquo; in Rootly but not in Jira. This typically happens because the mandatory fields required to transition an RDINC to &ldquo;Solved&rdquo; in Jira (***Rings**, **Impacted Asset(s)**, and **Root Cause Categorization***) have not been filled in.

When this occurs, a notification will appear in the RDINC &ldquo;Incident Information&rdquo; tab [image (1)], and a comment will be added to the incident [image (2)], alerting to complete the mandatory fields and move the Incident to &ldquo;Solved.&rdquo;

### ***What happens if an Incident is moved to &ldquo;Solved&rdquo; in Zendesk, but not in Jira?***
It may occur that an Incident is marked as &ldquo;Solved&rdquo; in Zendesk but not in Jira. This typically happens because the mandatory fields required to transition an RDINC to &ldquo;Solved&rdquo; in Jira (***Rings**, **Impacted Asset(s)**, and **Root Cause Categorization***) have not been filled in.

When this occurs, a notification will appear in the RDINC &ldquo;Incident Information&rdquo; tab [image (1)], and a comment will be added to the incident [image (2)]. The comment will notify the *Assignee *to complete the mandatory fields and move the Incident to &ldquo;Solved.&rdquo;
