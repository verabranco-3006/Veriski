**Impact Assessment** will be required based on the overall [**change risk**](https://outsystemsrd.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/RKB/pages/3819176170/What+does+an+ODC+Request+for+Change+look+like+in+Jira#Tab-%E2%80%9CGeneral%E2%80%9D) and also on [**5 risk vectors**](https://outsystemsrd.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/RKB/pages/3819176170/What+does+an+ODC+Request+for+Change+look+like+in+Jira#Tab-%E2%80%9CImpact-Assessment%E2%80%9D):

- 
**Availability &amp; Performance - **Might performing the Change negatively impact the Performance and/or Availability of customer applications? If such impact might exist, and whether it might manifest during the implementation and/or after the implementation of the Change, the risk must be identified and validated to ensure impact on OutSystems&rsquo; business is contained.

- 
**Security - **Might performing this Change increase the Security exposure of the service? Review should consider internal and external exposure, and focus on mitigating risks by lowering the exposure to acceptable levels.

- 
**Compliance - **Might performing the Change breach our policies &amp; procedures, or any legislation applicable to OutSystems? The review should focus both on whether the Change **can** be made and on **how to make it** to ensure such breaches don&rsquo;t occur.

- 
**Cost - **Might performing the Change change the costs OutSystems incurs with the service? The review should consider infrastructure, human resources, image costs, and potentially other costs as identified.&nbsp;

- 
**Market Fit - **Might performing this Change negatively impact the value to customers of our service, compromising our ability to retain current customers and/or acquire new ones? The review should consider the perceived value of our solution with customers, including features and byproducts, and focus on ensuring customers perceive the change as an improvement to the value of OutSystems.

The Risk of each vector is defined as a combination of the following attributes:

- 
**Impact** measures the effect of an incident, problem, or change on business processes. The result is often based on how service levels will be affected.

- 
**Likelihood** is a measure of the probability of an event like an incident, problem, or change to occur.
