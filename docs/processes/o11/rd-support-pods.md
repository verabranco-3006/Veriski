# What are R&amp;D Support PODs?
From time to time OutSystems Global Support Group can be overwhelmed by support cases, their backlog starts growing either because of a product problem, massive upgrade or lack of team capacity.

In order to mitigate this, and ultimately ensure that:

- 
Our customers get a good level of service

- 
R&amp;D doesn&rsquo;t get escalation of support cases opened months ago

- 
Global Support Backlog stops a growing trend and starts a reducing trend that Support can handle on their own.

R&amp;D temporarily lends a few developers to aid in troubleshooting Support Cases, until the Support Cases backlog returns to acceptable levels.

The R&amp;D Developers should be dedicated to this task, or at least be shared only with SRE rotation.

For each POD there will be a slack channel and Zoom meeting with all participants, to ease the communication.

The Global Support Backlog can be tracked in the following [Power BI](https://app.powerbi.com/groups/me/apps/c5d26f6f-df03-4c39-8160-96a50e90ae74/reports/94398930-04eb-432e-87e4-750bf98dafeb/ReportSectiona34798e094a717d27eec?redirectedFromSignup=1)
# What is expected from Support?
- 
Provide R&amp;D Developers access to Zendesk, with internal comments only permissions

- 
Provide training and support on how to use Zendesk

- 
Provide a curated list of Support cases, to be addressed exclusively in the POD, appropriate to the R&amp;D developers&rsquo; team

- 
Grouped by:

- 
Developer&rsquo;s team Product Areas

- 
Actionable: Not waiting for the customer (pending) nor escalated to R&amp;D

- 
If possible: Grouped by customer, Region, Common Problem

- 
L2 Support engineer(s) to work with the R&amp;D developers, communicate with the customer and discuss the support cases when needed.

# What is expected from R&amp;D Developers?
- 
Analyze the curated list of Support Cases to identify quick wins, address the quick wins first, either on your own or with the help of the Support Engineer.

- 
For each Support Case analyze it, look for workarounds or other solutions. Request more information if needed.

- 
Leave the answer as an internal comment in zendesk.

- 
Answers should follow the same format as replying to Support Cases escalated to R&amp;D.

- 
Once the customer replies, the R&amp;D Developer can pick the support case again, no need to wait for the Support engineer.

# What are the working hours for R&amp;D Developers?
- 
The R&amp;D developers maintain the regular business hours. Roughly 9h-18h

- 
Some R&amp;D developers have slightly different business hours, which will be respected during the Support POD.

# What is the expected Support Cases solution Rate for R&amp;D Developers?
- 
There is no expected solution rate.

- 
The R&amp;D Developer is not even necessarily able to solve support cases faster than a support engineer.

- 
The R&amp;D Developer is adding capacity to support, hopefully being able to solve support cases that would otherwise be left in the backlog.

- 
The R&amp;D Developer can and should request help from his team at any time.

# How long does the R&amp;D Developer stay in the Support POD?
- 
Support Pods that include R&amp;D Developers can happen until the Global Support Backlog reaches 400 tickets. Each individual POD defines its own exit criteria.

- 
It&rsquo;s up to the R&amp;D team to define when the Developer joins and leaves the POD.

- 
At least two weeks per developer is recommended, to avoid context switching and to actually make a difference on the Support Cases backlog.

- 
Maximum 4 weeks to avoid burnout

# Can Support Cases be escalated while a POD is ongoing?
- 
Yes, PODs may not contain all support cases that can be escalated to an R&amp;D team, so Support Engineers can escalate those support cases, and the R&amp;D team should address those normally.

- 
R&amp;D developers don't escalate support cases, if as a last resort they need to create a hotfix to solve a support case for example, they can do it without creating a support case in Jira.

- 
If during the investigation the R&amp;D developer finds that the support case is from another product area, the support case should be handed over to the Support Engineer and follow the normal process.
