# Tip for working Support Cases

# Priority 1: Unblock the customer
- 
Look first for a **workaround **to unblock the customer

- 
Create a Problem for follow-up (**don&rsquo;t fix it!!!**)

- 
Close the Support Case

If you are not able to find a workaround:

- 
Do the fix (under a Problem issue type)

- 
Inquire if the customer can wait for the **next release**

- 
Only provide **Hotfix as a last resort**

- 
Dealing with a feature **not entirely owned by your team**?

- 
**Join forces** with the developer in the support rotation from the other team

- 
The feature is **owned by your team but your team does not have enough expertise or needs helps from another O11 team**?

- 
Don&rsquo;t be afraid to ask for help outside you team but *ensure you gain the context along the way*

- 
E.g. do pair programming/rubber ducking sessions to absorb as much knowledge as possible

- 
Don&rsquo;t forget **you are still accountable and owner of the support case**

- 
All updates in the support case should be made by you!

- 
You are also responsible with maintaining good collaboration with regular syncs with whoever is helping out

- 
Don&rsquo;t leave without giving notice to whoever is helping

- 
Align expectations on what kind of help you require and how you will sync and gain context

- 
**There is not enough expertise inside the O11 cohort**

- 
Priority is always to unblock the customer so reach out outside the cohort!

- 
Try to ask for help in #rd-platform-dev

- 
Always give enough context so others understand the sense of urgency

- 
Set a reasonable timebox for a reply then proactively reach to the TL of the team you believe might have the expertise

- 
Try to reach an alignment on who can help you

- 
If not possible, escalate to the team&rsquo;s Engineering Manager

# Divide and Conquer
- 
**Minify **OMLs, WSDLs, Lifetime Stagings, etc

- 
Only work with the **minimum necessary** to reproduce the issue

- 
Troubleshoot and Debug is much easier if you are not compiling a 10MB OML for example

# Too many support cases on your backlog?
- 
Ask for help on  #[**gs-rd-incident-management-feedback**](https://outsystems.slack.com/archives/G01NSTM816Z)

- 
You can **challenge **a Support Case priority

- 
When the customer already accepted a **mitigation plan**, there is no need for a Support Case to remain with a **high** priority.

- 
Check the rules that govern Support Cases priorities [here](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/14uuvkRJRmfd6ziPH3LReXHuCVMIKM-V0NmI_fVwfaRw/edit#slide=id.g709c3399c6_2_39)

Learn more on how to create defects when you are working Support Cases [here](https://outsystemsrd.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/RCP/pages/730595517/Creating+defect+from+Support+Cases).
