# Method 5: AI-Enabled Organizational Capability

**Owner:** Vera Branco

**V2MOM Period:** Q2 2026 - Q1 2027 (4 quarters)

---

## Goal

Transform Process Engineering into an AI-enabled organization by developing individual capabilities, establishing career progression frameworks for the AI era, and industrializing managed processes through intelligent automation.

---

## Rationale

Process Engineering's long-term success requires a holistic transformation across three dimensions:

1. **Individual AI Enablement:** Every team member must be equipped to leverage AI tools in their daily work, increasing personal effectiveness and analytical capabilities.

2. **Career Development & Progression:** The Business Process Analyst profession is being redefined by AI. We must establish clear career paths, competency models, and growth frameworks that prepare our people for advancement in an AI-augmented world.

3. **Process Industrialization:** To scale our impact, we must codify process knowledge into automated, intelligent execution—moving from manual facilitation to self-service automation.

These three pillars are interdependent:
- **AI Enablement** makes individuals more effective today
- **Career Development** ensures sustainable growth and retention for tomorrow
- **Process Industrialization** scales our organizational impact and reduces toil

Without all three, we risk either burning out on operational toil (no automation), losing talent to attrition (no career paths), or failing to adapt to the AI transformation (no enablement).

## Submethods

### Submethod 5.1: Enable Individual Effectiveness Through AI Augmentation

**Objective:** Equip every team member with AI literacy and practical skills to leverage AI tools in daily workflows, increasing individual productivity and analytical depth.

**Activities:**

**Foundation (Q2 2026):**
- AI literacy baseline training for all team members
  - Prompt engineering fundamentals
  - AI tool landscape (Claude Code, ChatGPT, AI @ Product)
  - Understanding AI limitations and responsible use
  - Hands-on exercises for common workflows

**Daily Workflow Integration (Q2-Q3 2026):**
- Document AI-augmented workflows for core activities:
  - Meeting preparation (e.g., 1:1 prep, CAB prep, Operations Review prep)
  - Analysis and trend identification (e.g., incident patterns, RFC analysis)
  - Documentation writing and maintenance
  - Report generation and executive summaries
- Create team AI playbook with proven patterns and prompts
- Weekly AI experimentation sessions (share learnings, new techniques)

**Advanced Capabilities (Q3 2026 - Q1 2027):**
- Train team on AI @ Product platform basics (Skills, MCP, Agents)
- Develop skills for personal productivity (not just process automation)
- Build team capability to create and maintain simple skills
- Explore AI for strategic analysis and decision support

**Success Criteria:**
- 100% of team completes AI literacy baseline training by Q3 2026
- 80%+ of team actively uses AI in daily workflows by Q4 2026
- 5+ documented AI-augmented workflow patterns by Q1 2027
- Measurable time savings (target: 2-4 hours/week/person by Q1 2027)

**Jira Epic:** M5.1 - Individual AI Enablement

### Submethod 5.2: Build Career Paths & Develop Organizational Capability

**Objective:** Establish a comprehensive career progression framework for Business Process Analysts in the AI era and execute individual development plans for direct reports.

**Activities:**

**Career Framework Definition (Q2 2026):**
- Define competency model for Business Process Analyst roles:
  - **Associate Business Process Analyst** (0-2 years experience)
  - **Business Process Analyst** (2-4 years experience)
  - **Senior Business Process Analyst** (4-7 years experience)
  - **Principal Business Process Analyst** (7+ years experience)
- Map competencies across dimensions:
  - Process expertise (frameworks, governance, audit)
  - Technical capabilities (tools, automation, data analysis)
  - AI capabilities (AI tool usage, automation design, AI strategy)
  - Stakeholder management (communication, influence, partnership)
  - Strategic thinking (pattern recognition, systems thinking, organizational impact)
- Define advancement criteria and expectations for each level
- Document career progression paths and timelines

**Individual Development Plans (Q2 2026 - Q1 2027):**
- Create personalized development plans for each direct report:
  - Current level assessment
  - Target level and timeline
  - Competency gaps and development actions
  - Quarterly milestones and check-ins
- Assign stretch assignments aligned to growth goals
- Provide coaching and mentorship for career advancement
- Track progress and adjust plans quarterly

**Organizational Capability Building (Q2 2026 - Q1 2027):**
- Identify skill gaps across the team
- Create training and development programs to close gaps
- Build internal knowledge sharing rituals (e.g., brown bags, shadowing)
- Establish mentorship and peer learning opportunities
- Document career path framework in Confluence for transparency

**Success Criteria:**
- Career framework for Business Process Analysts documented and published by Q3 2026
- 100% of direct reports have active individual development plans by Q3 2026
- 80%+ completion rate on quarterly development actions
- At least 1 team member advances to next career level by Q1 2027
- Retention rate > 90% (voluntary attrition < 10%)

**Owner:** Vera Branco

**Jira Epic:** M5.2 - Career Development & Organizational Capability

### Submethod 5.3: Industrialize Processes Through AI-Powered Automation

**Objective:** Transition managed engineering processes from manual facilitation to intelligent, automated execution by codifying process knowledge into Process as Code blueprints and building technical implementation backlogs.

**Activities:**

**Discovery & Prioritization (Q2 2026):**
- Identify high-impact automation candidates across:
  - Change Management (RFC validation, routing, compliance checks)
  - Incident Response (triage support, retrospective prep, pattern analysis)
  - Problem Management (trend analysis, action item tracking)
  - Operational governance (CAB prep, reporting, evidence collection)
- Prioritize based on:
  - Impact (time saved, quality improvement, capacity freed)
  - Feasibility (technical complexity, data availability)
  - Alignment (V2MOM strategic priorities)
- Document automation opportunity backlog

**Blueprint Development (Q2-Q3 2026):**
- Draft technical "Process as Code" blueprints for top 3-5 automation candidates
- Define for each blueprint:
  - Process scope and workflow to be automated
  - Inputs (data sources, triggers, user inputs)
  - Logic (decision rules, validations, routing)
  - Outputs (actions, notifications, artifacts)
  - Integration requirements (Jira, Confluence, Slack, etc.)
  - Success criteria and measurement
- Validate blueprints with Engineering stakeholders
- Estimate effort and technical complexity

**Technical Platform Enablement (Q2 2026 - Q1 2027):**
- Build team technical capabilities on AI @ Product platform:
  - Skills framework and MCP architecture
  - Agent design and deployment
  - Integration patterns and APIs
- Deploy pilot skills to validate platform and team capabilities
- Establish development, testing, and deployment processes
- Create skill documentation and maintenance model

**2027+ Backlog Preparation (Q4 2026 - Q1 2027):**
- Convert blueprints into technical user stories and acceptance criteria
- Create prioritized backlog for post-V2MOM execution (Q2 2027+)
- Define ownership and resource allocation
- Establish metrics and success criteria for automation delivery

**Success Criteria:**
- 3-5 Process as Code blueprints completed and validated by Q4 2026
- At least 1 pilot skill deployed and operational by Q4 2026
- 100% of blueprints translated to technical backlogs by Q1 2027
- Post-V2MOM automation roadmap defined with clear ownership and timelines
- Core technical team (2-3 people) certified on AI @ Product platform by Q1 2027

**Owner:** TBD (Technical Lead for Process Automation)

**Jira Epic:** M5.3 - Process Industrialization & Automation
