# Method 5: AI-Enabled Organizational Capability

**Owner:** Vera Branco

**V2MOM Period:** Q2 2026 - Q1 2027 (4 quarters)

---

## Goal

Transform Process Engineering into an AI-enabled organization by developing individual capabilities, establishing career progression frameworks for the AI era, and industrializing managed processes through intelligent automation.

---

## Rationale

Process Engineering's long-term success requires a holistic transformation across three dimensions:

1. **Individual AI Enablement:** Every team member must be equipped to leverage AI tools in their daily work, increasing personal effectiveness and analytical capabilities.

2. **Career Development & Progression:** The Business Process Analyst profession is being redefined by AI. We must establish clear career paths, competency models, and growth frameworks that prepare our people for advancement in an AI-augmented world.

3. **Process Industrialization:** To scale our impact, we must codify process knowledge into automated, intelligent execution—moving from manual facilitation to self-service automation **for Engineering teams**.

These three pillars are interdependent:
- **AI Enablement** makes individuals more effective today
- **Career Development** ensures sustainable growth and retention for tomorrow
- **Process Industrialization** scales our organizational impact and reduces toil for Engineering teams

**Priority:** Process Industrialization for Engineering teams (M5.3a) is the **PRIMARY** focus. AI-augmented workflows for internal PE team (M5.1b) are **SECONDARY**—we build for others first, document internal patterns naturally as we adopt AI.

## Submethods

### Submethod 5.1: AI Adoption & Capability Building

**Objective:** Enable team to leverage AI for productivity gains through adoption, workflow documentation, and measurable time savings.

**Activities:**

**Foundation (Q2 2026):**
- AI literacy baseline training for all team members
  - Prompt engineering fundamentals
  - AI tool landscape (Claude Code, ChatGPT, Koda, AI @ Product)
  - Understanding AI limitations and responsible use
  - Hands-on exercises for common workflows
- **Target:** 100% team adoption by end of Q2 2026

**Internal Workflow Integration (Q2 2026 - Q2 2027):**
- Document AI-augmented workflow patterns for **internal PE team** activities (SECONDARY priority):
  - **Operations Review preparation** (HIGHEST ROI: Currently 2 days × 3 people = 6 person-days every 2 weeks. Target: Reduce to <2 person-days through AI-assisted data aggregation, trend analysis, report generation)
  - Meeting preparation (1:1 prep, staff meetings, skip-levels using existing skills like /prep1o1)
  - RFC review and automation candidate identification (support M2.4)
  - Incident & retrospective analysis (pre-RCA prep, pattern matching, maturity assessment support for M3.6)
  - Data analysis and trend identification (Jira queries, pattern recognition, correlation analysis)
  - Metrics report generation (automated summaries, trend narratives, exception highlighting)
  - Documentation writing and updates
  - Audit documentation preparation (seasonal, concentrated benefit)
- Create team AI playbook with proven patterns and prompts
- Weekly AI experimentation sessions (share learnings, new techniques)
- **Target:** ≥5 documented AI-augmented workflow patterns by Q2 2027

**Time Savings Measurement (Q2 2026 - Q2 2027):**
- Establish baseline time tracking for prioritized workflows (Q2 2026)
- Quarterly time savings surveys
- **Target:** 2-4 hours/week per team member saved by Q2 2027
- **Key Success Indicator:** Operations Review prep time reduced by 50-70%

**Success Criteria:**
- 100% of team actively using AI tools by end of Q2 2026
- ≥5 documented AI-augmented workflow patterns by Q2 2027
- 2-4 hours/week/person time savings by Q2 2027
- Operations Review prep time reduced from 6 person-days to <2 person-days

### Submethod 5.2: Career Development & Team Growth

**Objective:** Build career framework and develop team capabilities to ensure retention and growth.

**Activities:**

**Career Framework Definition (Q2-Q4 2026):**
- Define competency model for Business Process Analyst roles:
  - **Associate Business Process Analyst** (0-2 years experience)
  - **Business Process Analyst** (2-4 years experience)
  - **Senior Business Process Analyst** (4-7 years experience)
  - **Principal Business Process Analyst** (7+ years experience)
- Map competencies across dimensions:
  - Process expertise (frameworks, governance, audit)
  - Technical capabilities (tools, automation, data analysis)
  - AI capabilities (AI tool usage, automation design, AI strategy)
  - Stakeholder management (communication, influence, partnership)
  - Strategic thinking (pattern recognition, systems thinking, organizational impact)
- Define advancement criteria and expectations for each level
- Document career progression paths and timelines
- **Target:** Framework completed and approved by Q4 2026

**Individual Development Plans (Q2 2026 - Q2 2027):**
- Create personalized development plans for each direct report:
  - Current level assessment
  - Target level and timeline
  - Competency gaps and development actions
  - Quarterly milestones and check-ins
- Assign stretch assignments aligned to growth goals
- Provide coaching and mentorship for career advancement
- Track progress and adjust plans quarterly
- **Target:** 100% of team members with active IDPs executing quarterly milestones by end of Q2 2026 (quarterly tracking thereafter)

**Team Engagement (Q2 2026 - Q2 2027):**
- Monitor team engagement through Office Vibe eNPS or informal assessment mechanisms
- Small team size (3 people) prevents direct eNPS visibility to manager, requiring indirect measurement or informal pulse checks
- **Target:** Positive eNPS score (>0) by Q2 2027, trending toward >20 (favorable)
- Consider implementing informal assessment mechanisms (e.g., quarterly pulse checks, 1:1 engagement discussions) given small team constraints

**Success Criteria:**
- Career framework documented and published by Q4 2026
- 100% of direct reports have active IDPs with quarterly milestones by end of Q2 2026
- Positive team engagement (eNPS >0) by Q2 2027

**Owner:** Vera Branco

**Jira Epic:** M5.2 - Career Development & Team Growth

**Related V2MOM Metrics:** M5.2a (Career Framework Completion), M5.2b (IDP Execution), M5.2c (Team Engagement - eNPS)

### Submethod 5.3: Process as Code & Industrialization

**Objective:** Deliver automation solutions to Engineering teams by codifying process knowledge into Process as Code blueprints and deploying to production.

**Activities:**

**Discovery & Prioritization (Q2 2026):**
- Identify high-impact automation candidates **for Engineering teams** (PRIMARY priority):
  - Change Management (RFC validation, automation candidate flagging for M2.4, routing, compliance checks)
  - Incident Response (Event Management alert classification for M3.4 series, triage support, retrospective quality assessment for M3.6)
  - Problem Management (action item tracking automation for M3.2/M3.7, trend analysis)
  - Change-to-incident correlation (CFR correlation engine for M2.5)
- Prioritize based on:
  - Impact (time saved for Engineering teams, quality improvement, V2MOM metric support)
  - Feasibility (technical complexity, data availability, platform maturity)
  - Alignment (V2MOM strategic priorities - M2.4, M2.5, M3.2, M3.4, M3.6, M3.7)
- Document automation opportunity backlog

**Blueprint Development (Q2-Q4 2026):**
- Draft technical "Process as Code" blueprints for top 3-5 automation candidates
- Define for each blueprint:
  - Process scope and workflow to be automated
  - Inputs (data sources, triggers, user inputs)
  - Logic (decision rules, validations, routing)
  - Outputs (actions, notifications, artifacts)
  - Integration requirements (Jira, Confluence, Slack, etc.)
  - Success criteria and measurement
- Validate blueprints with Engineering stakeholders
- Estimate effort and technical complexity
- **Target:** 3-5 blueprints validated by Q2 2027

**Pilot Deployment (Q3 2026 - Q2 2027):**
- Deploy at least 1 automation solution to production
- Validate usage with Engineering teams
- Measure impact and effectiveness
- **Target:** ≥1 deployed to production with usage validation by Q2 2027

**Measurement Approach:**
- Track automation candidates through pipeline: Identified → Blueprint → Pilot → Production
- **Success:** At least 1 goes all the way from blueprint to validated production usage
- **Pipeline Health:** 3-5 at blueprint stage shows healthy pipeline for post-V2MOM execution

**Success Criteria:**
- 3-5 Process as Code blueprints validated by Q2 2027
- ≥1 automation solution deployed to production with usage validation by Q2 2027
- Post-V2MOM automation roadmap ready for execution
