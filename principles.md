# Leadership Principles

*Last updated: 2026-03-02*

## Core Beliefs

### On Leadership
- Transparency and humbleness are non-negotiable
- Active listening to catch the right feedback and act fast
- Balance sense of urgency with understanding everyone has limits
- Accountability over everything—build high-accountability teams
- Communicate safely and with empathy—put yourself in others' shoes
- Act according to company priorities, not personal preferences

### On Teams
- Autonomy with accountability—empower people to deliver value
- Outcomes over activities—no excessive paper trail, focus on big outcome-oriented buckets
- Simple planning beats complexity—Jira plan per initiative, that's it
- Can't please everyone, but must listen to all—engage with stakeholders, catch the right feedback
- High accountability is the foundation—create a culture where everyone owns their work

### On Engineering
- **Operational Excellence:** Codify best practices, full adherence, eliminate systemic risks, drive quality
- **Continuous Improvement:** Champion a Kaizen culture—use metrics and compliance as diagnostic tools, not gatekeepers
- **Automation First:** Leverage automation to accelerate delivery, reduce manual work, eliminate operational risks
- **Trusted Partnership:** Move from "policing" to enabling—transparency, integrity, clear accountability
- Rigorous, frictionless operational framework that minimizes systemic risk
- Invisible work must be made visible—formalize what's informal

### On Communication
- Transparency always—share context, reasoning, and impact
- Communicate safely and with empathy—understand others' perspectives
- Engage with multiple stakeholders—can't please everyone, but listen actively
- Act fast on feedback—catch the right signals and respond quickly
- Write to structure thinking, meet to align and decide
- Context before asks—never request without explaining "why"

## Decision Framework

When making decisions, I ask:
1. Does this align with company priorities?
2. Who are the impacted stakeholders? Have we listened to them?
3. Does this deliver the right value, or is it just paperwork?
4. Can we act fast while respecting limits?
5. Is this aligned with our V2MOM (Operational Excellence, Continuous Improvement, Automation First, Trusted Partnership)?
6. Does this move us from "policing" to "enabling"?

## Time Allocation

**Ideal week:**
- 60% Strategic work (process design, V2MOM, initiatives, process changes)
- 15% People management (1:1s, coaching, team health)
- 5% Execution (compliance work, audits)
- 5% Learning (staying current with industry practices)
- 15% Buffer/reactive time (unplanned work, stakeholder management)

**Reality check:** Review quarterly to ensure strategic work isn't being consumed by reactive demands

## Non-Negotiables

- **Biweekly 1:1s** with each of my 3 directs
- **Weekly team sync** to review V2MOM submethods (our north star)
- **Planning every 2-3 days** aligned with current priorities (not daily, not rigid)
- **Morning time blocks** for initiatives and strategic planning (protected deep work)
- **Afternoon time blocks** for stakeholder engagement and reactive work
- **V2MOM as north star** — all work aligned to Vision, Values, Methods, Obstacles, Measures
