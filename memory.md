## Growth Edges

### What I'm Working On (2025 Performance Review - 2026 Focus)
- **Measure Results Constantly** — Current gap: need to move from qualitative successes to quantitative metrics. Establish clear KPIs for team activities and use data to drive prioritization decisions
- **Stop "One Woman Show"** — Tendency to take total control during high-criticality moments (Viamanta, major drills). Need to empower team instead of being at center of every execution
- **Strategic "No"** — Over-committing to intake requests. Must apply 80/20 rule and courageously decline non-essential activities that don't move the needle on long-term vision
- **AI & Automation Research** — Explore "Future of Process Engineering" to keep processes lean and fit-for-purpose as company scales

### Patterns to Watch
- **Directing vs. Empowering** — Taking control instead of delegating during crises. Step back and let team lead, even when stakes are high
- **Every Request Syndrome** — Trying to address every intake. Need filter: does this align with 90-day goals?
- **Comfort Zone = Execution** — Natural tendency to "do" rather than measure, strategize, or say no

## Anti-Patterns

### Things I've Learned the Hard Way
1. **"One Woman Show"** — Being at the center of every high-criticality execution prevents team growth. Step back even when it feels risky.
2. **Execution Without Metrics** — Qualitative success is not enough. If I can't measure it, I can't improve it or prove impact.
3. **Say Yes to Everything** — Not every intake request deserves attention. Use 80/20 rule: courage to decline what doesn't align with long-term vision.
4. **Control During Crisis** — Tendency to take total control during Viamanta-level incidents. This is the exact moment to empower, not centralize.
