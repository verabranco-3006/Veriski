## Values (Decision Principles)

**Team V2MOM Values:**

- **Operational Excellence** — Achieve Operational Discipline by codifying best practices and full adherence to eliminate systemic risks and drive quality
- **Continuous Improvement** — Champion a Kaizen culture by leveraging objective metrics and compliance frameworks as diagnostic tools to identify systemic friction and drive incremental, data-backed enhancements
- **Automation First** — Drive maximum efficiency and velocity by leveraging advanced automation platforms and digital solutions to automate operational steps, accelerate delivery, reduce manual work, and eliminate unnecessary operational risks
- **Trusted Partnership** — Enable Shared Success through transparency, integrity, and clear accountability, moving from a "policing" role to a true Engineering enabler

## Decision Filter

When evaluating opportunities, does it:
- **Drive Operational Excellence?** (Codify practices, eliminate systemic risks)
- **Enable Continuous Improvement?** (Use metrics as diagnostic tools, drive data-backed enhancements)
- **Leverage Automation First?** (Reduce manual work, accelerate delivery, eliminate operational risks)
- **Build Trusted Partnership?** (Move from policing to enabling, transparency and accountability)

If not 3/4, probably not worth it.

## Key Obstacles to Navigate

**2026 V2MOM Obstacles:**

1. **Fixed-Date Regulatory Pressure** — FedRAMP deadlines (Method 1) create non-negotiable surge in design and documentation effort during H1. No flexibility on timelines.

2. **Skill-to-Code Gap** — Transitioning to "Process as Code" (Method 6) requires rapid mastery of AI @ Product framework APIs and agentic logic while maintaining current operations. Learning curve is steep.

3. **Platform Bifurcation Friction** — Technical disparity between O11 and ODC runtimes requires doubling discovery and design effort to maintain "Unified" standards (Method 5). Two platforms = 2x the work.

4. **Metric Trust & Data Fragmentation** — Fragmented data entry across Jira projects and lack of automated links between logs and version control risks diluting CPTO Metric accuracy (Method 4). Trust in data is foundation.

5. **Trust in Automated Governance** — Shifting from human reviews to system-enforced logic (Methods 2, 3, 5) requires absolute stakeholder confidence in technical data. Lack of trust risks regression to manual approvals, negating velocity and scalability gains.
