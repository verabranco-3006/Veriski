# GS Bi-Weekly Sync — Outcomes & Success Metrics (Draft)

*Working doc — pairs with `bi-weekly-global-support-sync.md`*
*Drafted: 2026-05-07*

---

## How to read this

Each outcome carries a **success metric** that measures the value delivered, not the activity. Activity completion (documents published, training delivered, rotations defined) is tracked through initiative trackers, not here.

Targets marked **(TBD baseline)** require a current-state measurement before commit. V2MOM/initiative anchors noted where applicable.

---

## 1. Document Service Incident vs Support Case separation

*Anchor: M3.3 Triage Models & OLAs*

≤5% mis-classification rate (Service Incident logged as Support Case or vice versa) per month, validated through post-incident review; zero classification disputes between GS and SRE per quarter.

## 2. Escalate and resolve 24x7 Engineering coverage for support cases

*Anchor: GS-SRE transition / M3.3 Triage Models & OLAs*

Mean time to engineer engagement on customer-impacting support cases is consistent across business / off-hours (gap ≤15%); zero escalation coverage gaps logged per quarter outside business hours.

## 3. Define O11 customer communication transition timeline

*Anchor: ir-comms-gs-sre-transition*

≥90% of milestones met on schedule; cutover communicated to customers no less than 4 weeks before transition.

## 4. Redesign O11/ODC GS-SRE swarming mechanism

*Anchor: ir-comms-gs-sre-transition*

MTTR for cross-platform incidents reduced by X% vs baseline (TBD baseline); ≥80% of cross-platform incidents follow the swarming SOP within 60 days of rollout.

## 5. Review full retrospective operating model

*Anchor: M3.5 Retrospective Transformation*

≥90% of mandatory retros follow the new model 30 days after rollout; action item closure rate aligns with M3.2 target (>80% for High/Critical within OLA/Deadline).

## 6. Establish OLAs for Problem Management and RCA key moments

*Anchor: M3.4 Problem Management + M3.3 Triage Models & OLAs*

≥80% of RPMs meet OLA targets; ≥80% of RCAs completed within OLA (M3.2 alignment).

## 7. Incident communication as a first-class citizen — automation and removal of ICL from Service Incident communication

*Anchor: M3.3 Triage Models & OLAs*

Time from incident declaration to first customer-facing message ≤X minutes (TBD baseline); zero Service Incidents requiring manual ICL handoff after rollout.

## 8. Create deterministic heuristics for GS to declare Service Incidents

*Anchor: M3.3 Triage Models & OLAs / M3.6 Event Management (TBC)*

≥95% of Service Incident declarations match heuristic criteria (validated post-incident); zero declaration disputes between GS and SRE per quarter after rollout.

## 9. Define notification mechanism for Engineering-detected incidents to Global Support

*Anchor: M3.1 Failure Management Governance*

≥95% of engineering-detected incidents notify GS within X minutes (TBD baseline); zero cases per quarter of GS first hearing about an incident from a customer.

## 10. Establish OLAs to handle Support Cases

*Anchor: M3.3 Triage Models & OLAs*

≥80% adherence per OLA tier; trend reported bi-weekly in Operations Review.

## 11. Set targets of escalation per Support Scope Level

*Anchor: M3.3 Triage Models & OLAs*

Actual escalation rate within ±5pp of target per scope level; quarterly review in Operations Review with corrective actions when out of band.

## 12. Establish a release visibility mechanism for GS

*Anchor: M3.4 Problem Management + Release Engineering*

≥95% of releases have GS-facing visibility on RPMs/bugs included; ≥X% reduction in "was this fixed?" support tickets within 6 months (TBD baseline).

## 13. Schedule Engineering Leadership session on CritSits

*Anchor: GS-SRE transition / Leadership engagement*

≥90% of follow-up actions from the session closed within agreed timelines; documented decisions tracked in operating cadence.
