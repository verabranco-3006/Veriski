## Talking Points

### 1. Address the Message Tone
João's response came across as paternalistic — explaining what a Jira status means when that was never the focus of PE's analysis. PE analyzed the list based on what was presented: "pending issues on our side." We did not look at Jira statuses; we looked at the substance of each issue to understand why it was blocked or pending.

**Key point:** If the goal was only "visibility," that wasn't clear from day one. The narrative presented multiple times was that GSSA "is not able to deliver due to Process Engineering."

### 2. Clarify Expectations
When a list of 24 issues is presented as "pending on Process Engineering," the obvious response is to analyze why they're pending. PE does not operate within GSSA's operating model and cannot interpret the list through that lens. If the goal was something other than accountability or unblocking, GSSA leadership needs to make that explicit upfront.

**Ask:** What was the actual goal of sharing this list? What did you expect PE to do with it?

### 3. Challenge the Narrative
GSSA leadership has stated multiple times — in meetings and outside them — that "GSSA is not able to deliver due to Process Engineering." This narrative is not aligned with the data. PE analyzed 24 issues and found:
- 10 (41.7%) are PE's primary responsibility
- 8 (33.3%) require SRE alignment
- 6 require other teams, including GS

**Key point:** PE has been responsive and collaborative on every request. Recent examples:
- O11 Connector GA: GSSA flagged blockers, PE immediately jumped in, redesigned priorities, and removed GS from critical path
- Internal Communication motion for Incident Response: Same pattern — PE responded at great expense to our own roadmap

**Ask:** Where is GSSA's accountability in solving these problems? What I see is GSSA pointing problems to PE and then stepping back, continuing to report that PE is the cause of lack of progression.

### 4. Define What "Pending" Means
If "blocked" in Jira is just a status and not an indicator of actual blockers, then what does "pending on Process Engineering" actually mean? PE cannot be expected to interpret GSSA's internal operating model. If there's no concrete ask, no formal proposal, and no clear action needed from PE, then what is PE expected to do?

**Example:** OLAs for R&D ODC Incident Response have been in discussion for 2 years. Only once was an analysis presented. We acted on those results, identified patterns together, and intervened with the team lead to level expectations. Then nothing happened. How is PE expected to invest capacity if no proposal is formally presented? Allocate capacity for what ask exactly?

### 5. Request Clear Asks Going Forward
If GSSA needs PE's involvement, the ask needs to be explicit:
- What problem are we solving?
- What is the specific request from PE?
- What does success look like?

PE is willing to collaborate — we've proven that repeatedly. But we cannot operate on vague "visibility" requests or absorb blame for lack of progression when there's no clear action for us to take.
