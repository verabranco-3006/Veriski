### 2. Point to PE's Track Record of Responsiveness

**Recent examples:**

What I see is a pattern: GSSA points problems to PE, then steps back. There's no joint ownership of solutions, no formal proposals, and no clear asks. Then GSSA reports that PE is the cause of lack of progression.

**Example:** OLAs for R&D ODC Incident Response have been in discussion for 2 years. Only once was an analysis presented. We acted on it together — identified patterns, intervened with the team lead to level expectations. Then nothing happened. How is PE expected to invest capacity if no formal proposal is presented? Allocate capacity for what ask?

**Ask:** Where is GSSA's accountability in driving solutions? What happens after a problem is pointed to PE? What is GSSA doing to move these forward besides reporting that PE is blocking?

If the list of 24 issues was just for "visibility" (as João Penedo explained), then what does "pending on Process Engineering" mean? PE analyzed the list because that's the obvious response when someone says "these are pending on your team." We looked at the substance of each issue to understand why it was blocked or pending.

If there's no concrete ask, no clear action needed from PE, and no formal proposal, then what is PE expected to do?

**Key point:** PE does not operate within GSSA's operating model. We cannot interpret the list through that lens. If the goal was something other than accountability or unblocking, that needs to be explicit upfront.

### 5. Set Expectations for Collaboration Going Forward
PE is willing to collaborate — we've proven that repeatedly. But collaboration requires:
- Clear asks: What problem are we solving? What is the specific request from PE?
- Joint ownership: GSSA cannot point problems and then step back. Solutions require shared accountability.
- Accurate narratives: Stop promoting the narrative that "GSSA cannot operate due to PE" when the data shows otherwise.

**Ask:** How do we move forward in a way that ensures both teams are accountable for driving solutions together?
