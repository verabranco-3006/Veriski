Hello team :slightly_smiling_face:

A few days ago we shared the consolidated Change Management direction with the VS Leads — the full picture of where we are taking Change Management in ODC across 2026 and 2027. We want to share that same direction with you, as selected SRE experts, and zoom into where your input matters most: Foundations, from Method 2 - Transforming Change Management.

We are building a centralized change catalog for ODC — one article per operation type, with the accepted risk classification defined at the operation level. If you have ever had to choose between multiple articles when linking an RFC, you know the problem: a rollback operation alone has 28 different catalog articles. We reviewed all ~1,700 2025 RFCs and mapped them to 61 distinct operation types, each with one proposed article.

**What this means for your role:**
• The RFC you execute links to one article — operation definition, parameters, and risk classification already set
• When what you are executing doesn't match any catalog type, that follows the out of catalog process — similar to the current workflow
• When a cataloged operation has inconsistent or inaccurate data, you will be able to formally flag it and stop execution until corrections are made
• Your judgment at execution time remains the check: does what I'm about to do match the article?

**Where we are and where we are going:**
Taxonomy validation with reviewers is underway — confirming which operation types belong in the catalog. The plan from here:
• End of Q2: agree on catalog content and taxonomy
• End of July: complete the catalog article review

That second milestone is where your input comes in.

**Where we need your help:**
We are reaching out to you specifically because we consider you the most experienced SREs on these operations. If there are others who should be part of this, let us know.

@Laura Ferreira is already planning working sessions with reviewers for next week — you are welcome to join, but most of what we are asking can be done offline. For each operation type: does the article accurately describe the operation as you execute it? Are parameters and risk classification right? Articles you sign off on become the single reference for implementers, reviewers, and approvers. We are just ensuring we keep momentum.

Any process changes that come out of this will be communicated with enough lead time for everyone to get ready. For now, we'd like your read on the direction and are counting on your participation when @Laura Ferreira reaches out. Thanks for your continued support! :rocket:
