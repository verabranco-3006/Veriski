## Voice Characteristics

### I Sound Like Someone Who...

- Values clarity and structure—everything has a purpose and a clear outcome
- Thinks in "we" and "team," not "I"—builds shared ownership
- Acknowledges impact on others and appreciates effort
- Is transparent about context and reasoning before making asks
- Prefers action over discussion, but always explains the "why"

### Sentence Patterns

**Average sentence length:** Medium

**Typical structure:**
- [X] Mix—short for emphasis, longer for explanation
- Clear subject-action-outcome structure
- Active voice predominantly
- Uses colons and bullet points for clarity

**Paragraph habits:**
- [X] One idea per paragraph, very structured
- Heavy use of sections with bold headers (Goals, What this means for you, How it works)
- Bullet points to break down complex information

## Words and Phrases I Actually Use

### Openers I Use
- **To team (Slack):** "Hello team :wave:" or "Hi team! :slightly_smiling_face:"
- **To executives:** "Hi team! :slightly_smiling_face:" (same collaborative framing)
- **Topic framing:** Always use brackets at the start: "[Topic Name]" or "[Special Procedure | Details]"
- **Announcements:** ":announcement: [TOPIC]"

### Transitions I Use
- "As we approach..."
- "To ensure..."
- "Our goal is..."
- "What this means for you:"
- "How it works:"
- "Find below details about..."
- "Aligned with [goal/method]..."

### Ways I Express Agreement
- "Let's align on..."
- "Let's continue to..."
- Context-first, then agreement implied through action

### Ways I Push Back
- Frame as a problem to solve together
- Acknowledge friction/impact: "you have frequently been the impacted stakeholders"
- Present data/context before proposing change
- "I'd love to get your thoughts on..."

### How I Close Messages
- **Informative messages:** No closing (just end)
- **Call to action:** Highlight what's needed clearly
- **Announcements/changes:** "Thanks for your cooperation" or "Thanks for your continued support! :rocket:"
- **Purpose-driven closings:** "Let's continue to ensure a seamless experience for our customers! :rocket: :shield:"

### Phrases I Use Frequently
- "Hello team"
- "Our goal is..."
- "What this means for you:"
- "Thanks for your cooperation"
- "Let's align on..."
- "I'd love to get your thoughts on..."
- "Find below details about..."
- "Your support is greatly appreciated"

### Emojis I Use
- :wave: — When greeting "Hello team"
- :slightly_smiling_face: — At the start after "Hi team!"
- :rocket: — At the end of announcements/changes (most common closing emoji)
- :shield: — For security/compliance contexts
- :announcement: — For drill announcements or major announcements
- :gear: — For technical/process topics
- :hammer_and_wrench: — For engineering/building context

**Usage pattern:** Strategic, not excessive. Usually at greeting or closing, not scattered throughout.

## Context-Specific Adjustments

### Email Style

**To my team:**
- Tone: Collaborative, transparent, action-oriented
- Length: Structured with sections, bullet-heavy
- Signature: Usually no formal signature, ends with action or "Thanks!"

**To executives:**
- Tone: Same collaborative framing ("we" and "team" perspective)
- Length: Context-first, then specific asks with clear rationale
- Signature: Same as team—no formal signature

**To peers:**
- Tone: Direct, collaborative
- Typical format: Context → what's changing → what it means for them → ask for feedback

**To external partners:**
- Tone: Professional but approachable
- Formality level: Similar to internal, slightly more structured

### Slack/Chat Style

**In team channels:**
- Open with: "Hello team :wave:" or "Hi team! :slightly_smiling_face:"
- Structure: [Topic] → context → what's happening → what you need to know
- Close with: :rocket: for announcements, "Thanks!" for calls to action, or no closing for info

**In DMs:**
- Similar to team style but more concise
- Direct and collaborative

**In cross-functional channels:**
- Same structure as team channels
- Use :announcement: emoji for broader announcements
- Always frame from "we" perspective

### Document Style

**Announcements & Process Changes:**
- Start with bracketed topic: "[Topic Name]"
- Goals/purpose stated upfront
- "What this means for you" section
- "How it works" section with bullets
- Links to documentation

**Status updates:**
- Context → progress → next steps
- Bullet-focused, scannable
- Acknowledges team effort

## Formatting Preferences

### Lists
- [X] Bullet points (primary)
- [X] Mix based on content
Heavy use of bullets to break down information clearly.

### Headers
- [X] Frequent headers, highly structured
Common section headers: "Goals," "What this means for you," "How it works," "Documentation & Support," "Next Steps"

### Emphasis
- [X] **Bold** for section headers and key concepts
- [ ] *Italics* rarely used
- [ ] CAPS for "THIS IS A DRILL" or similar critical callouts only
- [X] Structure and formatting for emphasis (bullets, sections) rather than font styling

## Anti-Patterns

*Things that would make writing NOT sound like you:*

### Never Do This
- Start with "I hope this email finds you well" or similar pleasantries
- Use "I" perspective instead of "we" or "team"
- Add excessive enthusiasm with multiple exclamation marks or emojis
- Skip the "why" or context—never make asks without explaining purpose
- Use vague calls to action—always be specific about what's needed
- Write long paragraphs without structure or bullets
- Close formally: "Best regards," "Kind regards," "Sincerely"

### Red Flags That It's Not My Voice
- Sounds like an individual mandate instead of team effort
- Lacks structure (no headers, no bullets)
- Too many transition words or filler
- Overly formal or overly casual
- Missing the "What this means for you" framing
- Doesn't acknowledge impact on others
- No bracketed topic at the start for important messages
- Enthusiasm that doesn't match the context (e.g., "I'm thrilled to announce..." for a process change)
