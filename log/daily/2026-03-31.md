## Notes

### GS+ Engineering Workshop - Day 2

**Status:** Solutions definition phase

Worked through 6 common challenges identified from the workshop:

**1. Operating Models to Handle Customer Disruptions**
- Service incidents and support cases conflated in current escalation model
- Severity models between Engineering and GS not aligned
- No formal alignment on when/how to involve Engineering in CritSits
- No formal process for 5-day SLA RCAs for CritSits
- Actions: Separate operating models, align CritSit definition, discuss 24x7 coverage

**2. Inconsistent Customer Communication on O11**
- SRE continues to manage customer communications during incident response
- Impact: Inconsistent quality, role confusion
- Actions: GS to take ownership with clear transition timeline

**3. Issues on O11 Operating Models for Higher Severity Incidents**
- GS escalates without formal warm hand-over
- Many times GS doesn't escalate incident, just the change they want executed
- Operational ownership blurred
- Actions: Define swarming protocol, establish warm hand-over process, clarify RACI

**4. Missing Collaborative Learning from Incidents and Support Cases**
- Post-mortems/RCAs siloed instead of collaborative
- No protocol for CritSit RCAs (Premium Support offer)
- Actions: Align on SRE involvement in RCAs, respect 5-day SLA, define clear ownership

**5. System-Wide Incidents Failing to Communicate Effectively**
- Time to acknowledge on Status Page higher than expected
- VS/EM as IC Leader seen as redundant intermediary
- Actions: Remove internal communication layer, SRE owns incident communication directly

**6. Higher Resolution Times Than Expected**
- Strong dependency on Engineering for ODC incident resolution drives higher MTTR
- Actions: Establish OLAs with response targets, align R&D metrics to V2MOM 7.1 (50% reduction, 12h target by EOY 2026), invest in GS ODC capabilities
